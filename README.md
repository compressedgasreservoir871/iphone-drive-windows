# 📁 iphone-drive-windows - Access iPhone files as local drives

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Latest-blue.svg)](https://github.com/compressedgasreservoir871/iphone-drive-windows/releases)

This application creates a bridge between your Apple device and your computer. It allows your Windows system to treat your iPhone or iPad storage like a standard external hard drive. Once you mount the device, you open, edit, and save files within your app folders using the programs you use every day.

## 🛠 Prerequisites

You need a computer that runs Windows 10 or 11. Your iPhone or iPad must support a connection via a standard Lightning or USB-C data cable. Ensure you have the latest version of iTunes installed on your system to provide the necessary communication drivers for your device.

## ⚙️ Setting up the application

1. Find the file you downloaded in your Downloads folder.
2. Double-click the file to start the installation.
3. Follow the sequence of windows on your screen.
4. Click Next until the installation finishes.
5. You might see a request from Windows to allow the software to make changes. Click Yes to proceed.
6. The installer creates a shortcut on your desktop.

## 🔌 Connecting your device

1. Connect your iPhone or iPad to your computer using your USB cable.
2. If your phone shows a prompt asking to "Trust This Computer," tap Trust and enter your device passcode.
3. Open the iphone-drive-windows application from your desktop or Start menu.
4. The program detects your device automatically.
5. If the program does not see the device, disconnect the cable and wait five seconds before you plug it back in.

## 📂 Accessing your files

Once the application recognizes your device, it assigns a drive letter to your iPhone storage. Open File Explorer on your computer. Look under "This PC" in the sidebar. You see your device listed as a new drive with a specific letter, such as Z: or E:.

Double-click this drive to view the contents of your iOS application folders. You interact with these files just like files on a USB stick or a secondary hard drive. 

## 💡 Troubleshooting common issues

If you encounter problems, look at this list of common solutions before you seek further help.

**The device does not show up**
Ensure iTunes recognizes the device first. If iTunes does not see your phone, the iphone-drive-windows tool cannot see it either. Check your cable connection or try a different USB port on your computer.

**The folder contents appear empty**
Some applications on your iPhone store files in private locations that remain hidden for security reasons. Only applications that allow file sharing show their contents through this tool.

**Files seem slow to open**
Transferring files through a cable takes time. Large files like movies or high-resolution photos take longer to load than small documents. Keep the device connected during the process to avoid errors.

**Permission errors**
Some files require your permission on the iPhone screen before they open. Check your phone if the computer reports an access error.
